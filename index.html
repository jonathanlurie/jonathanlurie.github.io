Hello, I am Jonathan Lurie,
a software developer and map maker.
I like to take photographs and build things.

I write code that creates images

I started in remote-sensing few years ago, working at Thales for the Pleiades satellite missions (ground segment, ESA/CNES), in Toulouse, France. Back then, I was mostly writing C++ code for Pleiades' image processing pipeline.

Since 2016, I moved to Montréal, Canada, to join Alan Evans' Montreal Center for Integrative Neuroscience, which is part of the Montréal Neurological Institute. Now, I focus mainly on 3D visualization of neurological datasets, and I use web technologies to make reasearcher-friendly apps.

In addition to that, I am also really into photography and I create maps.

Other (ir)relevant info:
  • I am French, from Bordeaux
  • I like cycling, hiking and rock climbing
  • I like to build things, even though I’m very bad at sawing
  • I may spend a few hours in a photo darkroom once in a while
